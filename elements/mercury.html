ATOMIC NUMBER: 80 ATOMIC MASS: 200.59(2)
Hg MERCURY
Mercury, atomic number 80, on the periodic table is a transition metal first used in ancient China in 1500 BC.
Mercury is the only metal that is a liquid at room temperature and pressure and has a very high surface tension.
Mercury is now known to be highly toxic, through throughout history was used both medicinally and for practical applications (thermostats etc).
Mercury is still useful to us. It conducts electricity and can be used in circuitry. Mercury vapor can also be found in lightbulbs.
